Types Of Coffee Beans

Arabica

Known for its smooth, nuanced flavor profile, Arabica beans are the most popular and widely cultivated type of coffee beans. They typically have a mild acidity and notes of fruit, chocolate, or nuts, making them a favorite among coffee connoisseurs.

Robusta

Robusta beans are prized for their strong, bold flavor and higher caffeine content compared to Arabica beans. They often have a more bitter taste with earthy or woody undertones. Robusta beans are commonly used in espresso blends for their crema-enhancing properties.

Liberica

Less common than Arabica and Robusta, Liberica beans have a distinctive flavor profile with fruity and floral notes. They are grown primarily in West Africa and have a unique, complex taste that sets them apart from other varieties.

Excelsa

Also known as Coffea excelsa, these beans are prized for their unique flavor profile, which often includes fruity, tart, and spicy notes. Excelsa beans are typically used to add complexity to blends or as a standalone component in specialty coffees.
